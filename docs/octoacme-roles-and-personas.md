# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers translate user and business needs into intuitive product experiences and interfaces. They ensure the product is user-friendly, accessible, and aligned with customer expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing to validate design decisions
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and UI specifications
- Collaborate with Product Manager and Developers to validate design feasibility
- Provide design QA support during execution and releases
- Maintain design systems and component libraries
- Ensure accessibility and usability standards are met

### Goals
- Create intuitive, user-centered product experiences
- Reduce friction and improve user satisfaction metrics
- Maintain design consistency across the product

### Typical Communication
- Weekly design reviews with Product Manager and Developers
- Design critique sessions and usability test results
- Design specs and style guides in project docs

### Interactions with Core Roles
- **Product Manager:** Partners closely to understand user needs, validate product requirements, and ensure designs align with product vision and success metrics
- **Developers:** Collaborates during implementation to ensure design feasibility, provides detailed specs and assets, and reviews UI implementation for design accuracy
- **Project Manager:** Participates in planning to estimate design effort, communicates design dependencies, and flags risks related to user experience

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Lead ensures the quality and reliability of product releases, championing a quality culture throughout the development lifecycle. They define testing strategies and serve as the final gatekeeper for release readiness.

### Responsibilities
- Define testing strategies and acceptance criteria
- Coordinate and execute manual and automated tests
- Manage bug triage and prioritization
- Advise on release go/no-go decisions
- Track quality metrics and test coverage
- Identify and document edge cases and quality risks

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality, reliable releases
- Minimize production defects and customer-impacting issues
- Improve test automation coverage and efficiency

### Typical Communication
- Daily standup participation for bug status and testing progress
- Weekly quality reports and test coverage metrics
- Release readiness assessments and risk summaries

### Interactions with Core Roles
- **Product Manager:** Collaborates to define acceptance criteria and success metrics, provides input on feature quality and user impact
- **Developers:** Works closely on test criteria, bug reproduction, and fix validation; coordinates automated test development
- **Project Manager:** Informs about release risks, testing timelines, and resource needs; participates in go/no-go decisions

---

## Customer Support

### Role Summary
Customer Support represents the voice of the user post-launch, gathering feedback, surfacing pain points, and ensuring customer success. They serve as a critical feedback loop for product improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Collect, analyze, and report user feedback and issues
- Help prioritize post-launch fixes and improvements
- Contribute to knowledge base and troubleshooting guides
- Provide early warning of systemic issues or user confusion
- Participate in UAT (User Acceptance Testing) when appropriate
- Document common customer workflows and use cases

### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction and reduce support ticket volume
- Provide actionable user insights to the product team
- Enable users to succeed with the product

### Typical Communication
- Weekly reports on trending issues and customer feedback
- Direct escalation of critical customer issues
- Knowledge base articles and FAQs

### Interactions with Core Roles
- **Product Manager:** Communicates customer pain points and feature requests, helps prioritize improvements based on user impact
- **Developers:** Works on reproducing and resolving customer-reported issues, provides real-world usage context
- **QA Lead:** Collaborates on bug reproduction, validates fixes from the customer perspective, and participates in release testing

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business needs and technical solutions, ensuring requirements are well-captured and translated into actionable plans. They facilitate alignment between stakeholders and the delivery team.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements
- Analyze business processes and recommend improvements
- Facilitate alignment between stakeholders and delivery team
- Create process flows, user stories, and requirement specifications
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business needs
- Identify and document business rules and constraints

### Goals
- Ensure clear, actionable requirements with minimal ambiguity
- Maximize business value delivery
- Reduce rework caused by misunderstood requirements

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and user stories
- Stakeholder interviews and requirement reviews
- Business process diagrams and data flow documentation

### Interactions with Core Roles
- **Product Manager:** Partners to clarify scope, acceptance criteria, and business context; helps translate business strategy into requirements
- **Project Manager:** Supports planning by breaking down requirements, identifying dependencies, and estimating effort
- **Developers:** Provides detailed specifications, answers questions about business logic, and validates implementation against requirements
